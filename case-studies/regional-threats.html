Regional Threat Monitoring

Bridging security intelligence with private sector decision-making and NGO operational resilience across regions.

📌 Context

Regional instability can have **direct commercial impacts** on logistics, supply chains, and humanitarian operations. I designed regional threat monitoring tailored to the operational requirements of global clients needing insights **beyond headlines**.

⚙️ Methodology

  • 📡 **Daily Incident Tracking:** Monitored cross-border risks in West Africa with a focus on supply chain bottlenecks and border security developments.
  • 📌 **NGO Access Risk Scoring:** Produced operational forecasts for **humanitarian agencies** navigating volatile areas.
  • 🔗 **Private Sector Risk Briefings:** Tailored to **logistics providers, insurance firms, and compliance teams**.
  • 🗺️ **Geospatial Analysis:** Mapped threat convergence zones affecting commercial and NGO operations.

📈 Results & Impact

  • ✔️ Supported **3 multinational logistics clients** to adjust routing and supply lines during periods of cross-border unrest.
  • ✔️ Provided **early warnings on conflict spillover risks** to regional NGO coordination bodies.
  • ✔️ Presented **risk briefings** to private security consultancies supporting investor decision-making on market re-entry timing.

✅ Why It Matters

Too often, regional threat monitoring is generic. My tailored approach combined geopolitical intelligence with private sector risk frameworks to inform not just what’s happening, but what to do about it for operations, compliance, and stakeholder engagement.
