📄 My Strategic Intelligence Cycle

This is the customized framework I use for strategic intelligence analysis, tailored for geopolitical and governance challenges in complex environments like West Africa.

🌀 1. Requirement Definition

I start by clarifying what decision or policy needs support.

This ensures the analysis is focused, relevant, and tied to real outcomes, not just reporting for reporting’s sake.

Example Question Framing:

  • ➡ “What risks could disrupt X country’s electoral process over the next 6 months?”
  • ➡ “Which stakeholders are likely to resist governance reforms in the Sahel region?”

📥 2. Collection Planning

I map out which sources are likely to provide insight:

  • OSINT: Academic papers, media monitoring, NGO reports
  • HUMINT: Trusted networks across sectors
  • Grey Literature: Internal reports, unpublished studies

Priority is given to source triangulation for verification.

🧹 3. Processing & Filtering

  • Noise vs Signal: I apply analytical filters to isolate reliable signals.
  • Bias Check: I actively challenge assumptions and run key assumptions tests to reduce the influence of bias in analysis.

📊 4. Analysis

Techniques I use:

  • Stakeholder Mapping: Influence, interests, and potential actions
  • Scenario Planning: Likely, worst-case, and wildcard futures
  • Risk Matrix: Likelihood vs. impact visualization

📤 5. Reporting & Recommendations

Outputs tailored to who’s receiving it:

  • Decision-makers → Strategic briefs
  • Policy teams → Detailed risk assessments with options analysis
  • Civic stakeholders → Simplified visuals and summaries

♻️ 6. Feedback Loop

Intelligence products don’t end on delivery.

Continuous feedback → Refinements → Updated analysis → More useful decisions.

📌 Why This Matters

Many organizations get overwhelmed by too much data. My framework ensures they get clarity, actionable choices, and timely insights to shape real-world decisions.
